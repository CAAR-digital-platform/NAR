Insurance Basics Last updated: March 2026

How insurance works

Insurance is a system where you pay a premium for financial protection against risks. If a covered event happens, the insurer pays for the loss according to the policy. Insurance transfers risk and provides financial security and peace of mind.

What is Insurance?

Insurance is a financial agreement that protects individuals or businesses from unexpected financial losses. In this agreement, the insured person pays a premium to an insurance company in exchange for coverage against specific risks such as accidents, illness, property damage, or death.

The fundamental principle of insurance is risk transfer: instead of bearing the full financial cost of a loss yourself, you pay a smaller, regular premium and the insurance company assumes the financial risk on your behalf.

How the Claims Process Works

Insurance works through a claims process. When a covered event occurs, the insured files a claim, and the insurance company compensates the insured or pays the service provider according to the policy terms and coverage limits.

  • A covered event occurs (accident, fire, theft, illness, etc.)
  • The policyholder notifies the insurance company promptly
  • The claim is submitted with supporting documentation
  • The insurer evaluates the claim and verifies coverage
  • Compensation is paid according to the policy terms and limits

Common Types of Insurance

There are several common types of insurance, each providing protection for different risks and situations:

  • Auto insurance — mandatory in Algeria; covers civil liability and optionally damage to your own vehicle
  • Home insurance — protects your property and belongings against fire, theft, water damage, and more
  • Business insurance — covers professional liability, equipment, and operating risks
  • Natural Disaster insurance (Cat-Nat) — mandatory in Algeria; covers earthquakes, floods, and other natural catastrophes
  • Transport insurance — covers goods in transit by road, air, or sea

Key Takeaways

  • Insurance is a risk transfer mechanism — you pay premiums, the insurer bears the financial risk
  • Claims must be filed promptly and with proper documentation
  • Every policy has coverage limits and exclusions — read them carefully
  • Some insurance types (auto, Cat-Nat) are legally mandatory in Algeria
  • Insurance provides financial security, peace of mind, and legal compliance

Why Insurance Matters

The main purpose of insurance is to provide financial security against unpredictable events. Key benefits include:

  • Financial protection — avoid devastating out-of-pocket costs from accidents or disasters
  • Peace of mind — knowing you are covered allows you to focus on what matters
  • Legal compliance — certain insurances are required by Algerian law
  • Business continuity — protects companies from events that could disrupt or end operations
  • Access to credit — lenders often require insurance coverage before approving loans
