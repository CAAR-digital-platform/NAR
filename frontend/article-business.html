Business Insurance Last updated: March 2026

How to protect your business

Business insurance protects companies from financial losses and risks. The business pays a premium, and the insurer covers losses from covered events. Understanding policy terms and coverage limits is essential for proper protection.

What is Business Insurance?

Business insurance is essential for protecting a company from risks and financial losses that may occur during its operations. It works by transferring the risk from the business to the insurance company. The business pays a premium, and in return, the insurer agrees to cover specific risks defined in the insurance policy.

Without adequate insurance, a single unexpected event — a fire, a lawsuit, or equipment failure — could threaten the entire financial health of a business.

How the Claims Process Works

If a covered event occurs, the business can file a claim to receive compensation for the financial losses. The process typically follows these steps:

  • Notify your insurance company as soon as the incident occurs
  • Document the damage or loss with photos, invoices, and reports
  • Submit a formal claim with supporting documentation
  • An assessor evaluates the damage and determines the compensation amount
  • Payment is issued according to the policy terms and coverage limits

Key Types of Business Coverage

Each insurance policy includes specific terms, coverage limits, and exclusions. Business owners should understand the main types of coverage available:

  • Multi-Risk Professional (MRP) — covers premises, equipment, stock, and professional liability in one contract
  • Professional Civil Liability (RC Pro) — protects against damages caused to third parties during professional activities
  • Machinery Breakdown — covers sudden failure of industrial or commercial equipment
  • Transport Insurance — protects goods in transit by road, air, or sea
  • Construction All Risks (CAR) — coverage for building projects from start to completion

Key Takeaways

  • Business insurance transfers financial risk from the company to the insurer
  • Understanding policy terms and exclusions is essential before signing
  • Coverage should be reviewed and updated as the business grows
  • Working with an experienced CAAR advisor helps tailor coverage to your specific activity
  • Proper documentation speeds up the claims process significantly

Choosing the Right Coverage

Working with an experienced insurance agent can help businesses choose the right coverage and adapt the policy to their specific needs. Consider the following when reviewing your business insurance:

  • Identify all risks specific to your sector and activity
  • Assess the value of your assets: buildings, equipment, stock, and vehicles
  • Ensure your liability limits are sufficient for potential claims
  • Review your policy at least once a year or after major changes in your business
  • Ask your CAAR advisor about bundled policies that may offer better value
